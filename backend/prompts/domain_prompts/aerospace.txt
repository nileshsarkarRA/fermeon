AEROSPACE ENGINEERING DOMAIN — COMPUTATIONAL ENGINEERING GUIDE:

Philosophy: Every aerospace part is a performance model. Geometry is the OUTPUT of physics, not the input.
Always derive wall thickness, cross-section, and taper from governing equations first.

KEY PHYSICS FORMULAS:
  # Nozzle throat area (rocket/jet) — conservation of mass through choked flow
  A_throat = mdot / (P0 * sqrt(gamma / (R_gas * T0)) * ((gamma+1)/2)**(-(gamma+1)/(2*(gamma-1))))
  # Nozzle expansion area ratio (isentropic, Mach 2.0 exit)
  area_ratio = 1.687  # A_exit / A_throat for M=2, γ=1.4  (isentropic table)
  # Wing spar wall — bending: wall_t = M_bending * c / (sigma_allow * I)  (simplified)
  spar_wall_t = max(sweep_chord / 40.0, 1.5)    # practical: chord/40, min 1.5mm
  fuselage_skin_t = max(cabin_dia / 200.0, 1.0) # shell: D/200, min 1mm (pressurised)

GEOMETRY TECHNIQUES:
- Nozzle / duct: loft between circular profiles at inlet, throat, and exit — NEVER extrude
- Wing aerofoil: spline the aerofoil section profile, then extrude/loft to tip with taper
- Fuselage: revolve a profile, shell() to correct skin thickness
- Brackets/lugs: derive from load path — material at stress concentration lines only

MANUFACTURING (AM metal — SLM/EBM):
  min_wall = 0.8   # mm  Minimum printable wall (SLM steel/Ti-6Al-4V)
  overhang_angle_max = 45.0  # degrees from horizontal — beyond this: add support or re-orient
  min_hole_dia = 1.0  # mm — below this, drill post-process

SIZING RULES:
- Wall thickness from pressure: wall_t = P * R / (sigma_allow / SF)    # thin-wall pressure vessel
- Minimum L/D ≥ 3 for any cylindrical section in flow path (prevents flow separation)
- Lightweighting pockets: remove material where stress < 10% of allowable
- All internal corners: r ≥ 2×wall_t to prevent notch fatigue
- Fastener holes: clearance = bolt_dia × 1.08 (ISO 273 medium fit)
